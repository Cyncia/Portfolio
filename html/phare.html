The Problem

In this age of digital tech, cameras are everywhere. Taking a picture is so easy now, but the real issue is how to share these photos in a convenient and efficient way. As an avid traveller, I love going on trips and taking photos. But, at the end of every trip, I spend hours uploading my images, distributing them with others, and downloading other people’s pictures. As a personal project with friends, I decided to create an app that provides a fast and convenient way for users to upload, distribute, and download images while also improving my technical skills.

  User Research

To better understand our users and guide us through the decision-making process, we researched issues that the general public might face when sharing photos and conducted a small scale survey within my local community about their photo-sharing experience.

To better understand our users and guide us through the decision-making process, we conducted a survey with six participants within our local community. They were asked several questions regarding their photo sharing habits and experiences with others. When asked, 4 participants responded that they had experienced previous dissatisfaction with friends and family members not sharing photos after a trip in a timely manner. These participants estimated that on average, it took up to a week for photos to be received from all members of the party, with upper bounds of up to 2-3 weeks in some cases. We also asked about potential factors that prevent users from wanting to share photos immediately. One response was that “when the trip is over, I don’t feel as motivated to upload pictures when I go back to my daily routine of work again”. Several others also mentioned that it was easy for them to “forget to do it” after a while.

*Note: the data from the survey may be biased as the participants were predominantly college students (whom we anticipated would be a large part of our target audience).

  Competitor Reserach

To understand why the listed user issues exist and persist, I researched current and alternative platforms many users use. There's a wide variety of services and platforms people use ranging from group messages to shared cloud services to private sharing apps.

Popular services (not pictured: cozi, tiny beans, 23snaps, telegram)

However, many of these platforms currently

  • require an account to be made or are connected to social media
  • gather the information of other users, such as email or phone number
  • require the user to specifically select which photos to upload
  • only enable the user to download images one by one
  • may compress the image quality

Although some platforms are easy to use and quick, they typically require an associated account [][][].

  Personas

To better understand who our users might be, I created 2 personas based off of the research that was conducted and from the insights I gained from reading through user forums. These personas represent different contexts in which various people might want to use an app to share photos with those close to them.

Meet Keith

Keith has worked as a biotech researcher for many years. He’s never gotten around to using social media apps and uses his phone to check the occasional email. His recent company retreat has given him several memories that he wishes to look back on, but his colleagues aren’t avid phone users either.

Meet Emily

Emily just started college and ended up taking many pictures with her family during her spring break. Soon, Emily and her siblings will be rushing back to their own busy lives, so Emily wants to easily exchange photos with them before they go.

Despite differing backgrounds of Keith and Emily, both share the common goal of easily sharing photos with those close to them. As photo sharing can be quite confusing and time consuming, many of participants I interviewed also expressed the desire to share photos in an easy and quick way.
