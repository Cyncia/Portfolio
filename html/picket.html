Picket Homes Internship

Improving the comps view for real estate agent and analyst users

OVERVIEW

At a high level, I worked on conducting research and addressing usability issues of their enterprise-facing real estate underwriting tool, Inertia Decision Science (DS).

I joined Picket Homes as the only UI/UX Design Intern in Summer 2021. Picket Homes is a B2B technology-driven real estate startup with a focus on becoming the world’s first consumer housing brand in the single family rental industry.

I’m unable to disclose specific details about my work due to a NDA agreement. Please contact me at cyncia@uw.edu for details.

PROJECT SCOPE

Inertia Decision Science is a proprietary web-based platform used to generate real estate return analyses for enterprise use.

When examining a specific property as a potential investment, users typically looks at comparable properties (comps) nearby.

I was tasked with working on the comps view within the Inertia DS platform.

Image Source

PROBLEM STATEMENT

How might we bring the comps view up to industry standards?

Defining “Industry Standard”

Real estate users work depends on how fast they can get lead properties out to their clients and Inertia DS serves to provide the tools to power faster single family rental acquisitions across markets.

Using the research I conducted as a foundation, I defined industry standard as the following to use as success metrics in evaluating whether the comps view would aid users in achieving their work goals.

  • Providing as much relevant and helpful information for users to determine whether a subject property has potential to profit
  • Eliminate reliance on external sources to gather information
  • As usable as current industry tools and competitors

SOLUTION

My exploratory learnings led me to understand the comps analysis process for real estate agent and analyst users and uncover gaps within the product that are hindering user tasks. As a result, I redesigned the comps view within the Inertia DS platform to help users analyze comps in a more efficient and user friendly way in accordance to their existing workflow.

PROCESS

Research Goals

01

Understanding the context and users

  • What are comps? How are they used? How do they fit into Inertia DS?
  • Who are the users? What are their goals and tasks?

02

Discover pain points and areas of opportunity

  • What issues does the current view suffer from?
  • How can the experience be improved?
